    • Waste sorting
    Putting items in the right bin is essential for efficient recycling. When non-recyclable items are mixed with recyclables, it makes the batch more challenging and costly to process and sometimes being rejected and sent to landfills instead. By sorting waste correctly, it ensures that resources can be used to their full potential.
    Where Recyclables End Up
    Once collected, recyclables are taken to undergo a sorting process. This involves separating different types of materials, such as plastic, glass, paper, and metals, so they can be processed. For example: Plastics are melted down and molded into new plastic products. Paper and cardboard are pulped and reformed into new paper products.
    By recycling, we conserve natural resources, reduce energy consumption, and minimize pollution in the production of new materials. Additionally recycling helps reduce the impact of waste in our environment,and contributes to a more sustainable future for our planet.
