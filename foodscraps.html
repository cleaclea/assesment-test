    Rubbish Food Scraps
    • FOOD SCRAP BINS
    Why are there food scrap bins?
    Food is a valuable resource, when sent to landfill it produces methane and CO2 which are harmful greenhouse gasses. Instead of being sent to landfill, food can be returned to the land in bio fertilizer to grow more food. There are climate and environmental benefits of recycling food scraps.

    How the food scraps are collected
    Your food scraps collection is the same day as your council rubbish or recycling service. Place your food scrap bin roadside the collection the night before your council collection day.

    What to put in your food scrap bins
    -Fruit and vegetable scraps
    -Bread pasta and rice
    -Meat bones and scraps
    -Fish bones and scraps
    -Egg shells
    -Nut shells
    -Coffee grounds
    -Fat/oils

    What cannot be put in your food scrap bin
    -plastic bags
    -Food wrapping
    -Cloth and clothing
    -soil or rocks
